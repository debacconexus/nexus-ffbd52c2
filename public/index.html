NODO — The Architecture Behind AIRS
Governed AI architecture for revealing institutional blindness
AIRS is the deployed implementation of NODO — a governed AI architecture developed through doctoral research at the University of Southern California. NODO defines how AI systems should reason across data schemas without fabricating, how governance gates should operate before inference executes, and how every AI action should leave auditable evidence.

The Knot — where separate schemas meet, where institutional blindness becomes visible, and where governance gates operate. NODO identifies the knot in your data before the AI crosses it.

Pre-inference governance — the IGM (Inference Governance Module) analyzes intent, applies NSI constraints, and sets token budgets BEFORE the responder executes. Governance is not inspected after the fact. It is enforced at the point of orchestration.

Audit receipts at every traversal — every cross-schema inference produces evidence: records scanned, rules executed, tokens consumed, RQ score, and timestamp. Governance becomes legible because the system can prove how it reasoned.
